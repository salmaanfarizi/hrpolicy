Introduction

HR Policy Manual

This Human Resources Policy Manual is provided as a central reference for all managers, supervisors and employees and applies to staff across all locations where the Company carries out its work.

The specific policies that follow promote the philosophy of ARS International Co. SPC with regard to standards of excellence; terms of employment; employee development; and employee services.

It may be necessary to change these policies from time to time to reflect changes in the workforce, employment trends, economic conditions and legislation. However, any changes in policy will be consistent with the Company's approach to:

  • Employing talented individuals whose creativity and imagination will support and contribute to achieving Company's business objectives
  • Communicating Company standards and expectations in all aspects of employment including performance
  • Valuing diversity, and assuring equal employment opportunity and a workplace where relationships are based on mutual respect
  • Treating all staff, workers, contractors and customers in a professional, non-discriminatory manner
  • Providing safe, effective working conditions
  • Providing competitive terms and conditions in our workplace market
Policy Updates: Any policy changes will be fully consulted on and communicated to all staff through normal communication channels. This Policy Manual will also be updated as necessary. This Manual applies to all jurisdictions globally.

Section 1.1

Recruitment Policy

Introduction

Effective recruitment and selection is central and crucial to the successful functioning of ARS International. It depends on finding people with the necessary skills, expertise and qualifications to deliver the Company's strategic objectives and the ability to make a positive contribution to the values and aims of the organisation.

Advertisement

Vacancies will generally be advertised in an appropriate newspaper or journal, and will not be confined to those media which, because of their particular source of applicants, provide only or mainly applicants of a particular group.

  • Advertisements will also be notified to the local job centre
  • The Company may, on occasions, decide to restrict advertisement to internal candidates only
  • In applying for posts, all candidates will be provided with a job description, details of the appropriate conditions of service and details about the Company
  • A brief statement about the appointment procedure will also be provided and, if possible, an indication of the date when interviews will be held

Job Description

The job description will include a list of the main duties and responsibilities of the post, together with an outline of the qualifications and experience which candidates are expected to possess.

Equal Opportunity: In drawing up the job description and conditions of service, the Company will ensure that no job candidate receives less favourable treatment than another on the grounds of disability, gender, race, religion or belief, age, sexual orientation, marital status, parental status, caring responsibilities or hours of work.

Candidate Requirements

  • Candidates will be asked to specify whether they have any disabilities, and whether there are any reasonable adjustments needed for them to attend an interview
  • All candidates with a disability who meet the essential criteria for a job will be interviewed, and considered on their merit
  • All candidates will be asked to declare on the application form whether they have ever been convicted of any criminal offence which cannot be regarded as 'spent'
  • Applicants will also be required to declare if they are related to any member of staff within the Company

Selection Process

A short-list of candidates will be drawn up for interview, based entirely on merit and suitability for the post but taking account of the Company's responsibilities in relation to the Disability Discrimination and Equalities Acts.

  • Reasonable notice will be given to ensure that candidates have sufficient time to prepare for and make the necessary arrangements to attend the interview
  • Canvassing of members of the Company is not permitted
  • No manager should be put into a position where he or she is asked to interview a person to whom they are related
Section 1.2

Induction Policy

General Policy Statement

ARS International believes that all new employees MUST be given timely induction training. This training is regarded as a vital part of staff recruitment and integration into the working environment. This policy, associated procedures and guidelines define the Company's commitment to ensure that all staff is supported during the period of induction, to the benefit of the employee and Company alike.

Aim

It is the aim of the Company to ensure that staff induction is dealt with in an organised and consistent manner, to enable staff to be introduced into a new post and working environment quickly, so that they can contribute effectively as soon as possible.

Benefits of Good Induction Practice

  • Enable new employees to settle into the Company quickly and become productive and efficient members of staff within a short period of time
  • Ensure that new entrants are highly motivated and that this motivation is reinforced
  • Assist in reducing staff turnover, lateness, absenteeism and poor performance generally
  • Assist in developing a management style where the emphasis is on leadership
  • Ensure that employees operate in a safe working environment
  • Reduce costs associated with repeated recruitment, training and lost production

Company's Commitment

The Company Human Resources Department / Head Office will:

  • Issue guidelines to familiarise managers and staff with the induction process
  • Maintain and update the Induction Policy
  • Provide a checklist for managers and staff to follow during the induction period
  • Deal with any problems promptly providing an efficient service for both managers and staff
  • Provide relevant formal training courses necessary to assist the induction process

Guidelines for Managers/Supervisors

Starting a new job is a demanding and often stressful experience. Quite apart from the obvious challenge of tackling new tasks, there is also the need to become accustomed to a new organisation, a new environment and new colleagues. The purpose of induction is to support new employees during this difficult period and to help them become fully integrated into the Company as quickly and as easily as possible.

First Day of Employment

Preparations should be made for the arrival of the new entrant well in advance. Most new employees tend to be concerned primarily with two matters:

  1. Whether they can do the job
  2. How they will get on with their new colleagues
Best Practice: It is important to introduce new employees to their workplace and colleagues at the earliest opportunity. A tour of the workplace should be arranged allowing the Company to be viewed as a whole and the recruit to see where he/she fits into the organisation.

Induction Checklist Items

Day One Tasks

  • Welcome and team introduction
  • Desk/workstation setup
  • IT access and equipment
  • Building tour
  • Emergency procedures

First Week Tasks

  • HR documentation review
  • Policy handbook briefing
  • Role expectations discussion
  • Department introductions
  • Initial training schedule

Section 1.5

Internet and Email Usage Policy

Introduction

This policy sets out the obligations and expectations on employees of the Company including contractors and temporary staff, who use the Company's IT facilities for internet and email purposes. IT facilities are provided to assist with day to day work. It is important that they are used responsibly, are not abused, and that individuals understand the legal, professional and ethical obligations that apply to them.

Authorization

No person is allowed to use Company IT facilities who has not previously been authorized to do so by the Company IT Department / Line Manager. Unauthorized access to IT facilities is prohibited and may result in either disciplinary action or criminal prosecution.

Legislation Compliance

All users shall comply with the relevant legislation. This includes:

Data Protection

Any information which the Company holds is potentially disclosable. This includes emails. Users need to be sure that they are not breaching data protection when they write and send emails.

  • Passing on personal information about an individual or third party without their consent
  • Keeping personal information longer than necessary
  • Email should be avoided when transmitting personal data about a third party

Computer Misuse

It is an offence to try and access any computer system for which authorization has not been given.

Copyright

It is an offence to copy software without the permission of the owner of the copyright.

Defamation

It is an offence to publish untrue statements which adversely affect the reputation of a person or group of persons.

Use of the Internet

The internet is provided primarily for business purposes. Individuals must follow any reasonable instruction issued by their line managers. Any personal use of the internet should be kept to a minimum and be outside working hours where possible.

Prohibited Internet Use

The following are strictly prohibited:
  • Visiting sites that contain obscene, hateful, or objectionable material
  • Downloading unauthorized software or files
  • Engaging in online gambling
  • Accessing social media sites excessively during work hours
  • Any activity that could bring the Company into disrepute
  • Which might reasonably be considered indecent, pornographic, obscene, defamatory, or libelous
  • Creating, disseminating, or storing discriminatory material

Use of Email

Email is provided primarily for business purposes. All emails sent and received on Company systems are the property of the Company.

Email Best Practices

  • Use clear, professional language
  • Include appropriate subject lines
  • Be mindful of confidential information
  • Do not send chain letters or spam
  • Do not open suspicious attachments
  • Report any suspicious emails to IT

Monitoring

The Company reserves the right to monitor and record internet and email usage without notice. This is done to ensure compliance with policies, protect against security threats, and maintain system performance.

Privacy Notice: Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using Company IT systems. All activity may be monitored and recorded.

Legitimate Access to Prohibited Material

In certain circumstances, employees may need to access material that would otherwise be prohibited (e.g., IT security staff investigating threats). Such access requires prior written authorization from management.

Section 2.1

Performance Appraisal Policy

Appraisal Policy

ARS International is committed to supporting every employee to reach their potential and achieve their personal goals, which in turn will assist the organisation to achieve its objectives.

The performance appraisal policy supports the performance appraisal scheme. The scheme is a formal process centred on an annual meeting of each employee and their line manager to discuss his/her work. The purpose of the meeting is to review the previous year's achievements and to set objectives for the following year.

Core Principles of the Appraisal Policy

  • The appraisal process aims to improve the effectiveness of the organisation by contributing to achieving a well motivated and competent workforce
  • Appraisal is an ongoing process with an annual formal meeting to review progress
  • The appraisal discussion is a two-way communication exercise to ensure that both the needs of the individual and of the organisation are being met
  • The appraisal discussion will review the previous year's achievement, and will set an agreed Personal Development Plan for the coming year
  • The appraisal process will be used to identify the individual's development needs and support the objectives of the Training and Development Policy
  • The appraisal process will provide management with valuable data to assist succession planning
  • The appraisal process will be a fair and equitable process in line with our Equality Policy

Performance Appraisal Implementation

  • Performance appraisal discussions will be held over a designated 4 week period on an annual basis
  • Line managers are encouraged to provide the opportunity for an additional 6 month verbal appraisal review, mid-year and other informal reviews as necessary
  • The discussion will be held in private. Information shared during the appraisal will be shared only with senior management
  • The appraiser (usually the employee's line manager) will be expected to have successfully completed appraiser training
  • All appraisal documents should be issued to both parties prior to the discussion

Performance Rating Scale

Exceeds ExpectationsAbove average performanceRegularly exceeds objectives

Key Performance Areas: Knowledge, skills and competencies to perform his/her job, achievement of agreed objectives, quality of work, teamwork and cooperation, communication skills, initiative and problem-solving ability.

Section 2.3

Disciplinary Procedure

Purpose

This procedure is designed to help and encourage employees to achieve and maintain standards of conduct and performance. It aims to ensure consistent and fair treatment for all.

Principles

  • The procedure is designed to be corrective rather than punitive
  • Employees will be informed of the nature of any allegation against them
  • Employees will have the opportunity to state their case
  • Employees have the right to be accompanied at formal disciplinary meetings
  • Employees have the right to appeal against any disciplinary decision

Stages of Disciplinary Action

StageActionDuration on File
InformalVerbal counseling and guidanceNot recorded formally
Stage 1First Written Warning6 months
Stage 2Final Written Warning12 months
Stage 3Dismissal or other sanctionPermanent

Gross Misconduct

The following are examples of gross misconduct which may result in summary dismissal without prior warnings:

  • Theft, fraud, or dishonesty
  • Physical violence or threats of violence
  • Serious harassment, bullying, or discrimination
  • Deliberate damage to company property
  • Serious negligence causing unacceptable loss or damage
  • Serious breach of health and safety rules
  • Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at work
  • Serious breach of confidentiality
  • Serious insubordination

Right of Appeal

Employees have the right to appeal against any formal disciplinary action within 5 working days of receiving notification of the decision. Appeals should be made in writing to the HR Department.

Section 2.4

Capability Procedure

Purpose

This procedure is designed to help employees who are having difficulty meeting the required standards of performance in their role. It provides a supportive framework to help employees improve while protecting the Company's interests.

Application

This procedure applies where an employee is not meeting the required standards due to lack of capability (skill, aptitude, or health) rather than misconduct.

Procedure Stages

  1. Informal Stage: Manager discusses concerns and provides support, training, or reasonable adjustments
  2. Formal Stage 1: Written improvement plan with clear targets and review period (typically 4-8 weeks)
  3. Formal Stage 2: Final improvement plan with extended support and monitoring
  4. Final Stage: If no improvement, consideration of redeployment, demotion, or dismissal

Support Measures

Training Support

  • Additional training courses
  • Mentoring or coaching
  • Job shadowing
  • Extended induction

Workplace Adjustments

  • Modified duties
  • Equipment or tools
  • Flexible working
  • Reduced workload temporarily
Medical Capability: Where capability concerns relate to ill health, the Company will seek occupational health advice and consider reasonable adjustments before taking any formal action.

Section 3.2

Prevention of Bullying and Harassment

Policy Statement

ARS International is committed to providing a working environment free from bullying and harassment. All employees have the right to be treated with dignity and respect. The Company will not tolerate any form of bullying, harassment, or victimization.

Definitions

Harassment

Unwanted conduct that has the purpose or effect of violating a person's dignity, or creating an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating or offensive environment. This includes harassment related to:

  • Age, disability, gender reassignment
  • Marriage and civil partnership
  • Pregnancy and maternity
  • Race, religion or belief
  • Sex and sexual orientation

Bullying

Offensive, intimidating, malicious or insulting behavior which makes the recipient feel upset, threatened, humiliated or vulnerable. Bullying is unlikely to be a single or isolated instance and includes:

  • Spreading malicious rumors
  • Insulting someone
  • Excluding or victimizing someone
  • Overbearing supervision or misuse of power
  • Unfairly blocking promotion or training opportunities
  • Making threats or comments about job security without foundation
Zero Tolerance: Bullying and harassment are treated as serious misconduct. Proven cases may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal.

Reporting Procedure

  1. Informal Resolution: If comfortable, tell the person that their behavior is unwelcome and ask them to stop
  2. Report to Manager: Report concerns to your line manager or HR
  3. Formal Complaint: Submit a written complaint for formal investigation
  4. Investigation: An independent investigation will be conducted
  5. Outcome: Appropriate action taken based on findings
Section 3.3

Whistleblowing Policy

Making a Disclosure in the Public Interest

The Company is committed to the highest standards of openness, probity and accountability. This policy provides a mechanism for employees to raise concerns about wrongdoing within the organization.

What Can Be Reported

This policy covers disclosures about:

  • Criminal offences
  • Failure to comply with legal obligations
  • Miscarriages of justice
  • Dangers to health and safety
  • Environmental damage
  • Financial fraud or mismanagement
  • Bribery or corruption
  • Deliberate concealment of any of the above

Protection

Protected Disclosures: Employees who make a disclosure in good faith will be protected from any detriment or victimization. The Company will not tolerate any retaliation against whistleblowers.

How to Raise a Concern

  1. Internal Report: Report concerns to your line manager, HR, or a senior manager
  2. Written Disclosure: Put your concerns in writing, including relevant details and any evidence
  3. Investigation: The Company will investigate all concerns raised
  4. Feedback: You will receive feedback on the outcome (subject to confidentiality constraints)

Confidentiality

All concerns will be treated in confidence and every effort will be made to protect the identity of the whistleblower. However, identity may need to be disclosed if required by law or for investigation purposes.

Equality Policy

ARS International is committed to promoting equality of opportunity for all staff and job applicants. We aim to create a working environment in which all individuals can make best use of their skills, free from discrimination or harassment.

Protected Characteristics

We will not discriminate on the grounds of:

Status & Orientation

Section 4.2

Drug and Alcohol Abuse Policy

Policy Statement

The Company is committed to providing a safe, healthy and productive working environment for all employees. The misuse of drugs and alcohol can affect work performance, safety, attendance and the Company's reputation.

Rules

  • Employees must not be under the influence of alcohol or drugs during working hours
  • The consumption of alcohol on Company premises is prohibited except at authorized events
  • The use, possession, distribution or sale of illegal drugs on Company premises is strictly prohibited and may result in criminal prosecution
  • Prescription medication that may affect performance must be declared to management
Consequences: Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at work is considered gross misconduct and may result in summary dismissal.

Support

The Company recognizes that alcohol and drug dependency are treatable conditions. Employees with such problems are encouraged to seek help. Support may include:

  • Confidential counseling
  • Referral to specialist services
  • Time off for treatment
  • Modified duties during recovery
Section 4.3

Health and Safety Policy

General Statement

ARS International is committed to ensuring the health, safety and welfare of all employees, contractors, visitors and others who may be affected by our operations. We will comply with all applicable health and safety legislation.

Employer Responsibilities

  • Provide and maintain safe systems of work
  • Ensure equipment and materials are safe
  • Provide adequate training, information and supervision
  • Conduct risk assessments and implement control measures
  • Maintain a safe working environment
  • Provide adequate welfare facilities

Employee Responsibilities

  • Take reasonable care of your own health and safety
  • Take reasonable care not to endanger others
  • Cooperate with the Company on health and safety matters
  • Use equipment and substances in accordance with training

Manual handling activities should be avoided where possible. Where unavoidable:

  • Risk assessments must be conducted
  • Training must be provided
  • Mechanical aids should be used where appropriate
  • Employees must follow safe lifting techniques

Fire Safety

  • Fire exits must be kept clear at all times
  • Employees must know the location of fire exits and assembly points
  • Fire drills will be conducted regularly
  • Fire fighting equipment must not be tampered with

Contractors

All contractors working on Company premises must comply with our health and safety policies and procedures. Contractors must be briefed on relevant hazards and emergency procedures before starting work.

Onboarding Process

Meaning/Purpose

Onboarding refers to the collective processes and activities of integrating new employees. The onboarding period begins when the new employee accepts an offer and joins the organisation. It includes preparing for, enabling, engaging, and supporting the new employee.

Scope & Eligibility

Onboarding Process Components

  • Design reporting system & assign mentor
  • Fill required forms (Employee Joining form, PF form, Bank account opening form, Nomination form)
  • Brief on Employee handbook including all HR policies
  • Send welcome mail to all departments

2. Induction Program

Organisation Introduction

  • History & Background
  • Vision & Mission
  • Key People
  • Organisation Chart

Your Role

  • Expectations
  • Deliverables
  • Targets & Accountability
  • Growth Opportunities

3. Buddy Up Program

A new employee's onboarding is greatly enhanced by setting up a buddy for the employee. A buddy is not the supervisor, but rather a colleague who can provide guidance and support during the initial period.

Annexure I - C

Annexure III - F

Employment of Relatives Policy

Policy Statement

The Company does not prohibit the employment of relatives but has guidelines to prevent conflicts of interest.

Definition

A relative is any person who is related by blood or marriage, or whose relationship with the employee is similar to that of persons who are related by blood or marriage.

Guidelines

  • Relatives of persons currently employed may be hired only if they will not be working directly for or supervising a relative
  • Employees cannot be transferred into a reporting relationship with a relative
  • If a relative relationship is established after employment, the individuals concerned will decide who is to be transferred
  • If that decision is not made within 30 calendar days, management will decide
Disclosure: Each employee has a responsibility to keep his/her supervisor informed of changes relevant to this policy, such as becoming a relative of another employee through marriage.

Annexure III - I

Whistle Blower Policy & Anti-Sexual Harassment Policy

Whistle Blower Policy

A Whistle Blower is an individual employee, channel partner, business associate, or stakeholder who reports any kind of improper conduct or unethical behavior within the organization.

Scope

This policy encourages all Whistle Blowers to report any kind of:

  • Fraud, corruption, or misappropriation
  • Violation of company policies
  • Misuse of company assets
  • Conflicts of interest
  • Any illegal or unethical conduct

Protection

Whistleblower Protection: The Whistle Blower/Complainant will be protected from any retaliation. Their identity will be kept confidential to the extent possible.

Anti-Sexual Harassment Policy

The Company is committed to providing a workplace free from sexual harassment. All forms of sexual harassment are prohibited and will be dealt with severely.

What Constitutes Sexual Harassment

  • Unwelcome physical contact or advances
  • Sexually colored remarks or jokes
  • Showing pornography or offensive material
  • Making sexual demands or requests
  • Any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature

Reporting & Redressal

  • Complaints can be made to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)
  • Complaints will be investigated confidentially
  • Appropriate action will be taken against offenders
  • False complaints may also result in disciplinary action
Zero Tolerance: Sexual harassment is treated as serious misconduct. Proven cases will result in strict disciplinary action up to and including dismissal.

Annexure IV - A

Long Distance Travel Policy

Meaning/Purpose

Travel for the purpose of this policy shall mean all travel undertaken by any employee outside of his/her permanent business location, for the purpose of the Company's business including training and rewards & recognition (R&R) trips.

Scope & Eligibility

  • Applicable to all permanent employees on the rolls of the Company
  • Not applicable to contractual staff
  • Covers both domestic and international travel

Guidelines

  • Travel should be undertaken only after exploring all other communication options (e.g., teleconferencing/videoconferencing)
  • Travel advance will be given for company business travel
  • Travel requisition number is mandatory for all individual travel
  • A maximum of 2 international trainings will be allowed per employee within a calendar year
  • Strict disciplinary action will be taken against any employee violating the policy

Sanction Authority

Nature of TravelAuthorized By

International TravelDirector / Board

Travel Arrangements

Ticketing

  • Travel desk is appointed to minimize costs and ensure service quality
  • International air ticket booking needs to be done 15 days before actual travel
  • Any exception requires Authorized Signatory approval
  • If self-booking is cheaper by 5%, employee can book and claim reimbursement

Annexure IV - B

Short Distance Travel Policy

Purpose

This policy covers local and short-distance travel for business purposes within the city or nearby areas.

Travel Arrangements

  • Company vehicles may be used where available
  • Public transport expenses will be reimbursed
  • Personal vehicle usage will be compensated per kilometer
  • Taxi/ride-hailing expenses for business travel will be reimbursed

Policy Features

Mode of TransportReimbursement
Company VehicleNo additional reimbursement
Personal Vehicle (Car)Per kilometer rate as per policy
Personal Vehicle (Motorcycle)Per kilometer rate as per policy
Public TransportActual expenses with tickets
Taxi / Ride-hailingActual expenses with receipts

Claims Process

  1. Record: Maintain a log of all business travel
  2. Collect: Keep all receipts and tickets
  3. Submit: Submit claims monthly with supporting documents
  4. Approval: Get manager approval for reimbursement
  5. Settlement: Reimbursement processed with salary

Personal / Leisure Travel

Personal travel combined with business travel must be clearly separated. All personal expenses should be paid by the employee and not claimed as business expenses.

Important: Hotel accommodation expenses must be supported by proper receipts. Any personal expenses should be paid for by the employee.
